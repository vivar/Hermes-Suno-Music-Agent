# SuperGrok Video Reference (xAI)

## Overview
SuperGrok Video leverages xAI's advanced multimodal capabilities for generating short video clips directly from text or image prompts. It emphasizes creative interpretation, strong narrative understanding, and cinematic quality with minimal prompting effort.

## Prompt Structure
- Write in natural, story-like sentences
- Include camera direction, mood, and pacing
- Reference film techniques when helpful (e.g., "in the style of a Wes Anderson tracking shot")
- Keep prompts concise but vivid

**Example Prompt:**
"A lone guitarist stands on an empty stage under a single spotlight. The camera slowly circles around them as they begin to play. Warm golden light, subtle dust particles in the air, intimate and emotional atmosphere. Smooth cinematic motion."

## Best Practices
- Use descriptive, film-inspired language
- Specify emotional tone and lighting mood
- Include subtle environmental details (wind, light flares, reflections)
- Generate short, focused clips rather than long sequences
- Leverage image inputs for character consistency across shots

## Strengths
- Excellent at understanding narrative intent and emotional beats
- Strong cinematic composition and lighting
- Good at stylized or surreal concepts
- Fast iteration and creative flexibility
- Integrates well with other xAI tools (SuperGrok image + video)

## Things to Avoid
- Overly technical or rigid camera instructions
- Extremely long or complex multi-shot scenes in one generation
- Heavy reliance on text overlays (can be unreliable)

## Advanced Tips
- Generate key visual moments first as images, then convert to video
- Use for "lyric visualizer" style clips that match emotional peaks in the song
- Combine with music-production.md timing guidance for beat-synced motion
- For music videos: create a series of short emotional vignettes rather than one continuous shot
- Reference songwriting.md for matching visual storytelling to lyric themes
