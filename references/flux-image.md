# Flux Image Generation Reference

This guide covers best practices for prompting Flux text-to-image models (local via ComfyUI, Forge, or online platforms).

## Overview

Flux is currently one of the strongest open image models. It excels at following complex instructions, realistic anatomy, detailed environments, and artistic styles. It responds particularly well to long, descriptive natural language prompts.

## Prompt Structure Recommendations

Flux performs best with this general flow:

1. **Subject description** (detailed)
2. **Environment and setting**
3. **Pose / Action**
4. **Lighting and mood**
5. **Art style / aesthetic**
6. **Camera and composition**
7. **Technical quality** (optional)

## Example Prompt Format

```
A mysterious figure standing on a foggy mountain ridge at dawn, wearing a long dark coat, wind blowing through their hair, dramatic golden light breaking through the clouds, cinematic composition, highly detailed, atmospheric, 8k
```

## Best Practices

- Use full sentences and natural descriptions — Flux loves context
- Be very specific about lighting, time of day, and atmosphere
- Include clothing details, textures, and materials
- Mention camera angles and shot types when composition matters
- Flux handles complex scenes and multiple subjects well
- Describe textures and materials relevant to the scene or theme

## Quality Boosters

These tend to work well with Flux:
- highly detailed
- intricate details
- sharp focus
- realistic
- cinematic lighting
- atmospheric
- photorealistic
- 8k

## Things Flux Excels At

- Realistic human anatomy and faces
- Complex scenes with multiple elements
- Text in images (signs, shirts, etc.)
- Consistent character appearance across generations
- Natural lighting and shadows
- Vehicle and mechanical details

## Things to Avoid

- Very short keyword-style prompts (Flux prefers descriptive text)
- Overly conflicting instructions
- Extremely abstract concepts without visual grounding

## Advanced Tips

- Flux is excellent at understanding relationships between objects and people
- You can describe camera lenses (wide angle, 35mm, telephoto)
- It responds well to film photography references (Kodak Portra, cinematic stills)
