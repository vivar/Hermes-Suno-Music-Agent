# Midjourney Video Reference (v6+ / Video Features)

## Overview
Midjourney's video capabilities (via --video parameter or integrated video tools) allow users to generate short motion clips from images or prompts. It produces artistic, stylized motion with strong aesthetic control, making it excellent for creative and cinematic music video segments.

## Prompt Structure
- Start with a strong Midjourney-style image prompt
- Add motion and camera instructions
- Use parameters like --ar, --stylize, and --video
- Keep the core visual language consistent with still image prompting

**Example Prompt:**
"A lone wanderer standing on a cliff edge at sunset, dramatic sky, cinematic composition --ar 16:9 --stylize 750 --video"

## Best Practices
- Generate a high-quality still image first, then apply video motion
- Use clear camera direction language (slow pan, gentle orbit, push in)
- Keep motion relatively simple and elegant
- Leverage Midjourney's strong aesthetic sense for stylized results
- Use consistent seed or character reference when creating multiple clips

## Strengths
- Beautiful artistic and cinematic motion
- Strong stylistic coherence with Midjourney's image aesthetic
- Excellent for moody, atmospheric, or painterly video clips
- Good integration with existing Midjourney workflows
- Strong composition and lighting from the start

## Things to Avoid
- Highly realistic human action or complex physics (better suited to Veo 3 / Kling)
- Rapid or chaotic motion
- Overly long generations

## Advanced Tips
- Create a full music video by generating a series of keyframe images, then adding subtle motion to each
- Use for stylized performance shots or dream-like sequences
- Pair with references/midjourney-image.md for consistent prompting language
- For music videos: generate abstract visualizers and emotional vignettes rather than literal performance
- Match motion pacing to song energy using notes from arrangement.md and mixing.md
