# Nanobanna Image Generation Reference

This guide covers best practices for prompting Nanobanna, a remote AI image generation service.

## Overview

Nanobanna is a remote image generation platform known for producing high-quality, detailed images with strong prompt adherence. It is particularly popular for its balance of realism, artistic quality, and ease of use through simple text prompts.

## Prompt Structure Recommendations

Nanobanna responds well to this general flow:

1. **Main subject** (detailed description)
2. **Environment / Setting**
3. **Action / Pose**
4. **Lighting and atmosphere**
5. **Art style or aesthetic**
6. **Camera / Composition**
7. **Quality enhancers**

## Example Prompt Format

```
A mysterious figure standing on a foggy mountain ridge at dawn, wearing a long dark coat, wind blowing through their hair, dramatic golden light breaking through the clouds, cinematic composition, highly detailed, atmospheric, 8k
```

## Best Practices

- Use clear, descriptive natural language
- Be specific about lighting, mood, and atmosphere
- Include details about clothing, textures, and materials
- Mention camera angles and shot types when composition is important
- Nanobanna handles complex scenes and multiple subjects well
- Add quality boosters at the end of the prompt for stronger results
- Describe textures and materials relevant to the scene

## Quality Boosters

These tend to work well with Nanobanna:

- highly detailed
- intricate details
- sharp focus
- cinematic lighting
- atmospheric
- photorealistic
- 8k
- ultra realistic
- masterpiece

## Things Nanobanna Excels At

- Realistic human features and expressions
- Complex scenes with good composition
- Atmospheric lighting and mood
- Consistent character appearance
- Natural environments and detailed backgrounds
- Vehicle and object details

## Things to Avoid

- Overly vague or short prompts
- Conflicting style instructions
- Extremely abstract concepts without visual grounding

## Advanced Tips

- Nanobanna benefits from longer, more descriptive prompts
- You can reference specific lighting conditions (golden hour, dramatic side lighting, neon, etc.)
- Film photography references (e.g., cinematic stills, Kodak Portra) can improve realism
- Adding camera lens references (35mm, wide angle, telephoto) can help with composition
