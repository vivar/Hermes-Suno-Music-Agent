# Qwen Image Generation Reference

This guide covers best practices for prompting Qwen text-to-image models (local or via Qwen platforms).

## Overview

Qwen's image models respond well to detailed, natural language descriptions. They handle complex scenes, cultural elements, and artistic styles effectively.

## Prompt Structure Recommendations

Best results usually come from this order:

1. **Main Subject** (who/what is the focus)
2. **Environment / Setting**
3. **Action / Pose**
4. **Lighting & Atmosphere**
5. **Art Style / Medium**
6. **Camera / Composition**
7. **Quality boosters** (optional but helpful)

## Example Prompt Format

```
A mysterious figure standing on a foggy mountain ridge at dawn, wearing a long dark coat, wind blowing through their hair, dramatic golden light breaking through the clouds, cinematic composition, highly detailed, atmospheric, 8k
```

## Best Practices

- Be specific with details (clothing, lighting, time of day, mood)
- Use natural sentence structure rather than just keyword lists
- Qwen handles long, descriptive prompts well
- Specify camera angles when composition matters (low angle, wide shot, close-up)
- Mention lighting (golden hour, dramatic lighting, neon, etc.)
- Reference textures and materials relevant to the scene or theme

## Quality Boosters

Add these at the end for better results:
- highly detailed
- sharp focus
- intricate details
- realistic
- cinematic lighting
- 8k

## Things Qwen Excels At

- Realistic human features and expressions
- Natural environments and rural scenes
- Vehicle and mechanical details
- Atmospheric lighting
- Cultural and regional aesthetics

## Things to Avoid

- Overly abstract or surreal prompts without clear grounding
- Extremely long run-on sentences
- Conflicting style instructions
