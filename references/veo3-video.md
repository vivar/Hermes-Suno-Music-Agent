# Veo 3 Video Reference (Google)

## Overview
Veo 3 is Google's state-of-the-art text-to-video model, known for exceptional realism, physics simulation, and cinematic quality. It handles complex scenes, natural human motion, and detailed environments with high fidelity. Best used for high-production-value music video segments.

## Prompt Structure
- Write detailed, film-like scene descriptions
- Specify camera movement, lighting, and atmosphere clearly
- Include subject actions and environmental interactions
- Use professional cinematography language

**Example Prompt:**
"Golden hour on a vast empty highway. A vintage convertible drives toward the camera with the top down. Warm sunlight creates long shadows and lens flares. Slow tracking shot moving backward as the car approaches. Dust kicked up from the tires, cinematic color grade, ultra-realistic motion and reflections."

## Best Practices
- Use rich, specific descriptions of lighting, weather, and texture
- Explicitly describe camera moves (dolly, crane, tracking, handheld)
- Include subtle details like fabric movement, hair, reflections, and particle effects
- Generate 4–8 second clips for maximum quality and control
- Leverage image-to-video mode with high-quality starting frames

## Strengths
- Best-in-class realism and physics (water, fire, fabric, hair)
- Outstanding human movement and facial expressions
- Superior lighting and cinematic color science
- Strong prompt adherence for complex scenes
- Excellent for dramatic, emotional, or high-production music video shots

## Things to Avoid
- Overcrowded scenes with too many moving elements
- Rapid cuts or very fast camera motion in a single generation
- Small text or fine UI elements
- Extremely long clips (quality degrades past ~8–10 seconds)

## Advanced Tips
- Create a "shot list" from your lyrics first, then generate individual clips
- Use for key emotional or chorus moments where maximum realism matters
- Combine with references/music-production.md for timing and energy matching
- For music videos: generate hero shots (performance, landscape, narrative) and edit in post
- Pair with sound-design.md when planning how movement interacts with the track
