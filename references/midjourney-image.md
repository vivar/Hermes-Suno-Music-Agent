# Midjourney Image Generation Reference

This guide covers best practices for prompting Midjourney (v6 and newer), whether through Discord or the official web interface.

## Overview

Midjourney is one of the most popular remote AI image generators, known for its strong artistic quality, beautiful lighting, and excellent prompt interpretation. It excels at cinematic, stylized, and highly aesthetic images.

## Prompt Structure Recommendations

Midjourney responds well to this general flow:

1. **Subject description** (detailed)
2. **Environment and setting**
3. **Action / Pose**
4. **Lighting and mood**
5. **Art style / aesthetic**
6. **Camera and composition**
7. **Quality and style parameters** (optional but powerful)

## Example Prompt Format

```
A mysterious figure standing on a foggy mountain ridge at dawn, wearing a long dark coat, wind blowing through their hair, dramatic golden light breaking through the clouds, cinematic composition, highly detailed, atmospheric, 8k
```

## Best Practices

- Midjourney works very well with natural, descriptive language
- Be specific about lighting, atmosphere, and mood
- Use comma-separated phrases or full sentences — both work effectively
- Reference artists, film styles, or photographic techniques when you want a specific look
- Add parameters at the end of the prompt (e.g., `--ar 16:9 --stylize 750 --v 6`)
- Midjourney handles complex scenes and multiple subjects very well
- Describe textures, materials, and lighting in detail for stronger results

## Quality Boosters

These commonly improve Midjourney results:

- highly detailed
- intricate details
- cinematic lighting
- atmospheric
- photorealistic
- masterpiece
- 8k
- ultra realistic
- beautiful composition

## Things Midjourney Excels At

- Cinematic and filmic lighting
- Artistic and stylized aesthetics
- Strong composition and mood
- Realistic human features with good anatomy
- Complex scenes and environments
- Consistent artistic style across generations

## Things to Avoid

- Overly short or vague prompts
- Too many conflicting style references in one prompt
- Expecting perfect text in images without specific prompting

## Advanced Tips

- Use parameters like `--ar` (aspect ratio), `--stylize`, `--chaos`, and `--v` (version) to fine-tune results
- Artist references (e.g., "in the style of [artist]") work extremely well
- Film photography references (Kodak Portra, cinematic stills, anamorphic lens) can dramatically improve realism and mood
- You can use `--no` to exclude unwanted elements
- Midjourney v6 is particularly good at understanding relationships between objects and people
